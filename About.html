Cotton (Kapas) is one of the most important soft fiber crops cultivated primarily in hot and dry climates. It is typically grown as a Kharif crop, sown during the monsoon season, usually between June and July. The cotton plant not only provides the raw material for the textile industry but also produces cotton seeds, which are used for oil extraction, and cottonseed cake, which is widely used as animal fodder. Gujarat, our home state, holds the distinction of being the largest cotton-producing state in India.

Peanut (groundnut) is an important leguminous crop that is widely cultivated across the world, especially in warm and dry climates. It is typically grown during the Kharif season, which begins with the onset of the monsoon in June and July, and is harvested around September to October. Peanuts are highly nutritious, being a rich source of protein, healthy oils, vitamins, minerals, and dietary fiber. They are used in various forms, such as snacks, peanut butter, and to extract peanut oil, which is popular in cooking. The cultivation of peanuts requires proper soil preparation, timely sowing, adequate irrigation, and effective pest and disease control to ensure good yields. Guru Krupa Khol & Beej Bhandar proudly offers high-quality Bhindi (Okra) seeds suitable for excellent crop yield. Bhindi, also known as Okra or Lady Finger, is a popular and nutritious vegetable widely grown in India. It grows best in warm climates with well-drained loamy soil and a temperature range of 25°C to 35°C. The ideal sowing seasons are June–July for the Kharif crop and February–March for the summer crop. Around 4 to 5 kg of seed is used per acre, with a recommended spacing of 30 cm between rows and 15 cm between plants. The crop benefits from 8–10 tons of organic manure per acre and proper irrigation. Flowering begins around 35 to 40 days after sowing, and harvesting starts by 45 to 50 days. Regular harvesting every 2–3 days ensures tender and marketable pods. Bhindi has strong market demand and provides continuous income, making it a valuable crop for farmers. Generally, Dudhi is ready for harvesting 60–70 days after sowing. With proper use of organic manure, irrigation, and disease control, the crop yields can be significantly improved. Guru Krupa Khol & Beej Bhandar provides top-quality seeds for Brinjal (Ringna/Ringli) – a widely consumed and economically important vegetable in India. Brinjal grows well in a variety of soils, especially fertile loamy or sandy loam soil with good drainage. The ideal temperature for cultivation ranges from 20°C to 30°C. It can be cultivated in all three seasons – Kharif, Rabi, and Summer – depending on irrigation facilities. The seed rate is approximately 200–250 grams per acre, and the spacing should be maintained at 60 cm between rows and 45 cm between plants. The crop starts flowering in about 45–50 days, and harvesting begins after 60–70 days from transplanting. Brinjal plants produce continuously, allowing for regular harvests every 7–10 days. With proper nutrient management and pest control, farmers can achieve excellent yields. Brinjal is always in high demand in vegetable markets due to its regular household consumption and versatility in cooking. GURU KRUPA KHOL & BEEJ BHANDAR provides high-quality Soyabean Beej (soybean seeds) for farmers looking to achieve better yields and healthier crops. Soyabean is a major Kharif crop in India, grown for its rich oil and protein content. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained black or loamy soil. Popular high-yielding varieties like JS-335, JS-9560, and MACS-1407 are available, maturing in 90 to 110 days. The recommended seed rate is 60 to 80 kg per acre, depending on whether the land is irrigated or rainfed. To ensure strong germination and plant health, seeds should be treated with fungicides and biofertilizers like Rhizobium and PSB. Though primarily a rainfed crop, 1–2 irrigations during flowering and pod formation can significantly boost yield. Proper fertilization with urea, SSP, and potash enhances productivity. Pests such as stem flies and girdle beetles should be managed with the right insecticides. With timely sowing (mid-June to early July) and good management practices, farmers can expect a yield of 14–16 quintals per acre. Harvesting is done when the pods dry and turn brown, ensuring high-quality grains. For trusted soybean seeds and expert farming guidance, farmers rely on GURU KRUPA KHOL & BEEJ BHANDAR.
